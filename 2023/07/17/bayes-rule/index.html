Motivating example

Let's say you're a doctor. A woman with no symptoms comes into your office after she's tested positive for breast cancer. Let's say the prevalence of breast cancer at her age is 111 in 100100100. Imagine we have a test that is 90%90\%90% accurate when the person has cancer and 91%91\%91% accurate when they don't have cancer.

We often refer to 90%90\%90% as the "sensitivity" (since it's how sensitive the test is to the disease). We call the 91%91\%91%, "specificity", since it's how specific the test is for the disease.

The woman is stressed out that she tested positive. She asks you, "How likely is it that I have breast cancer?" Is the answer 10% or 90%? Many people make the mistake of saying 90%90\%90%. The actual probability is 10%10\%10%. This article will explain why, and how to avoid this mistake.
