Examples

Say we are given this set of coordinates: {(1.3,0.8),(3.2,3.5),(5.6,6.4),(8.5,7.7)}\{(1.3, 0.8), (3.2, 3.5), (5.6, 6.4), (8.5, 7.7)\}{(1.3,0.8),(3.2,3.5),(5.6,6.4),(8.5,7.7)}. Then we can either do the arithmetic by hand (from the last equation) or open julia and give it the vectors (some output omitted):

julia> y = [0.8; 3.5; 6.4; 7.7]
julia> X = [1.3 1; 3.2 1; 5.6 1; 8.5 1]
julia> inv(X' * X) * X' * y
2-element Vector{Float64}:
 0.9628
 0.1228

Which gives us our desired line of best fit: y=0.962823x+0.122874y = 0.962823x + 0.122874y=0.962823x+0.122874.
